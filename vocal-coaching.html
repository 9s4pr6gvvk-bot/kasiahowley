01About the Coaching

A functional approach,
grounded in research.

Kasia is a vocal coach with extensive experience running a private practice and training performers at some of the UK's leading drama schools. She holds a BA (Hons) in Musical Theatre from ArtsEd London, and is completing her Master's in Vocal Pedagogy with the Voice Study Centre - researching vocal fatigue and translating the science into practical, everyday guidance.

"My approach is functional: efficient, sustainable technique that holds up under a strenuous schedule and a demanding stage."

Mentored in the Estill method by one of the North's leading Estill Master Trainers, her research-informed approach helps clients meet the demands of a strenuous schedule and high-intensity performance - always within a safe, supportive and individualised environment.

03Add-on Treatment

Vocal Manual Therapy.

As professional voice users, we should give our voice the same attention and care we give to our bodies. Vocal manual therapy is a specialist treatment that targets the muscles surrounding the larynx - predominantly the jaw, neck and larynx region.

Kasia is a fully qualified vocal manual therapist, trained under the guidance of the Voice Care Centre in London. Every client has different needs, so she draws on a range of techniques to support each individual.

Myofascial release Soft tissue mobilisation Traction Gentle manipulation

A feeling of release

An overall sense of ease and freedom when using the voice.

Greater mobility

Increased mobility and flexibility of the larynx itself.

Enhanced quality

A noticeably clearer, more enhanced overall voice quality.

Relief from fatigue

Meaningful relief from the effects of vocal fatigue.

Less tension

Reduced muscular tension through the jaw, neck and larynx.
